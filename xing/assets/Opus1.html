The work is based on the client's request and the generation of an architectural planning scheme, called “Riverside Promenade”.
The client wanted to create a place near the food court to attract daytime traffic on the North Shore and provide a place for night-time food court customers to relax during the day. (And a chance to learn more about the history and culture of the North Shore Brisbane area) The project, which focuses on plants, animals, and the history of the port on the North Shore, hopes to provide an interactive cultural venue for visitors and citizens before night falls.
The project includes a historical timeline documenting important historical events related to the region. Creative seats and cultural signs, so that visitors can understand the local culture at rest. Interactive areas, including interactive murals and electronic message boards, allow young visitors to deepen their understanding of the region's cultural history during their visit.
The successful establishment of the project could increase the nighttime activity of the food court, attract more young people to Brisbane's North Shore during the day and provide a good place for citizens and tourists to take social photos.
