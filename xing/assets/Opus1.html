The work is an architectural proposal called "Riverside Promenade" based on the client's request.
Riverside Promenade is a recreational landscape project on Brisbane's North Shore, inspired by the North Shore's nature and history. The client wanted to create a venue near the food court to attract the North Shore traffic during the day, providing a place for customers in the night food court to rest and relax during the day. (and the opportunity to let visitors learn more about the local history and culture of North Shore/Brisbane)
Project Theme and Objectives:
Theme: The project focuses on the flora, fauna and boat harbour history of the North Shore, providing a culturally rich retreat for visitors and citizens.
The goal: to increase the activity of the food court at night, attract more young people to Brisbane's North Shore during the day, and provide a great place for citizens and visitors to take social photos.
Project Highlights:
Timeline Landscape: A timeline set along the riverside promenade records important historical events related to the area while serving as a unique photo check-in point.
Creative seating and cultural symbols: The riverside promenade area is equipped with seats resembling boats, animals or plants, and accompanied by relevant cultural interpretation signs, providing visitors with a dual experience of knowledge and rest.
Interactive Areas: Interactive areas equipped with interactive murals and electronic information boards provide young people with a new and fun interactive experience while deepening their knowledge of Brisbane's North Shore culture and history.
Desired Results: Through the Riverside Promenade project, it is expected to activate the flow of people in Brisbane's North Shore, strengthen the transmission of local culture and history, and provide citizens and visitors with a high-quality public space that integrates leisure, learning and socializing.
